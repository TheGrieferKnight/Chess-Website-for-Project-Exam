Discovered Attacks in Chess: A Strategic Guide

How to Use Discovered Attacks to Create Winning Combinations and Control the Board

By: Fabian Steineck

February 28, 2023

chess strategy games chess tactics

A discovered attack in chess is a strategy that involves moving one piece to reveal an attack from another piece behind it. This is frequently accomplished by moving a bishop, knight, or queen to reveal a previously blocked attack from a rook or queen.

A discovered attack can be a very effective tactic because it allows a player to attack two pieces at the same time, putting pressure on the opponent and potentially gaining material or positional advantage. For example, if a knight is moved to reveal a queen's attack on an opponent's rook, the opponent must either move or lose the rook, potentially allowing the player to gain material or positional advantage.

In chess, there are two kinds of discovered attacks: simple discovered attacks and double discovered attacks. When one piece is moved to reveal an attack from another piece, this is referred to as a simple discovered attack. When a piece is moved, it reveals an attack from two other pieces at the same time, which is known as a double discovered attack.

Players must be able to identify potential targets and plan their moves accordingly in order to use discovered attacks effectively. They must also be aware of their own and their opponent's piece positions, as well as the potential consequences of their moves.

Finally, discovered attacks are an important tactic in chess and are frequently used to gain material or positional advantage. Players can improve their chances of winning and becoming more successful in their games by mastering the art of the discovered attack.
